Biography

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam, full name Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, was an eminent Indian scientist and the 11th President of India. He was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, and passed away on July 27, 2015.

Kalam was a brilliant aerospace engineer and played a pivotal role in India's civilian space program and military missile development. He is often referred to as the "Missile Man of India" for his contributions to India's missile technology.

In 2002, he became the President of India, serving in this prestigious role until 2007. During his presidency, he continued to inspire the nation, particularly the youth, with his vision for a developed and self-reliant India.

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam was a respected scientist, visionary leader, and an excellent teacher who dedicated his life to education and the advancement of technology. He passed away while doing what he loved most—interacting with students. His legacy continues to inspire countless individuals, and he remains a symbol of excellence, humility, and patriotism in India and beyond.
